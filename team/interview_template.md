# Team Interview Template

Use this quick interview to help assign BlueMark FPV hackathon work. Short answers are fine. The goal is to understand what each person can own, where they need support, and what should not land on their plate.

## Questions

1. What is your name, and how much time can you realistically contribute during the hackathon?
2. What coding experience do you have? List languages, frameworks, or projects that are relevant.
3. How comfortable are you with Python? Any experience with OpenCV, image processing, or video processing?
4. How comfortable are you with frontend work, dashboards, charts, UI state, or React/Vite-style apps?
5. How comfortable are you with backend/API work, TypeScript, Node.js, Express/Fastify, or data flow wiring?
6. How comfortable are you with signal processing, RF concepts, math, statistics, detection logic, or noisy data?
7. Do you have defense, drone, EW, FPV, robotics, or related domain knowledge that could help the demo or pitch?
8. How comfortable are you with Git/GitHub: branches, commits, pull requests, merge conflicts, and reviewing changes?
9. How comfortable are you with design, demo flow, slides, storytelling, presentation, or judging Q&A?
10. Do you prefer owning a task independently, pairing with someone, or working from clear step-by-step guidance?
11. What parts of the BlueMark FPV demo sound most interesting to you?
12. What parts do you definitely not want to own?
13. What are your biggest time constraints, scheduling issues, or risk factors during the hackathon?
14. What is one concrete thing you think you can deliver by the end of the event?

## AI Summary Prompt

After answering the questions, paste your answers into ChatGPT/Codex with this prompt:

```text

Return:
```
