## Refinements to project framing

### Drone video feed perspective

The video feed is **first-person from the drone (FPV)**, not third-person observer. Confirmed by Arpit; Nicholas had been unsure.

### Detection approach — multi-signal cross-validation (NEW)

Identification is no longer single-signal (marker only). The pipeline should fuse multiple signals into a confidence score:

- **Visual drone-type classifier.** YOLO fine-tuned on public drone datasets, identifying friend-vs-foe by visual class. Arpit's framing.
- **Authenticated OSD marker** ("invisible glyph"). Dynamically rotating in real ops (~20s) to prevent enemy reverse-engineering. For demo, a simpler static or counter-based glyph is acceptable.
- **Bird's-eye stitching from cooperating FPV feeds.** Nicholas's idea — stitch multiple friendly FPV feeds into a synthetic top-down view, identify drones not in the friendly information graph as candidate foes. Viability depends on whether friendly drones actually share telemetry — SME question for Birger.
- **Acoustic.** Birger has prior knowledge that acoustic sensor coverage is abundant. Out of MVP, in pitch.
- **Infrasound.** Arpit's add. Out of MVP, in pitch.
- **Fiber-optic.** Birger noted: expensive, sparse. Pitch-only reference.
- **Crypto ESP32.** Sebastian found ~$10 blockchain-based unique drone identity hardware. **Open scope question** — original pitch was minimal-hardware; this introduces hardware. Resolution required before architecture lock.

### Confidence + risk-zone logic (NEW)

Operator dashboard semantics expand beyond the four-state taxonomy:

- Show confidence percentage (0–100%) per feed.
- Map confidence + zone risk to action recommendation:
  - **High confidence friend (~80%+):** don't engage, don't jam.
  - **High confidence foe (~≤20%):** engage / jam permitted.
  - **Mid (~50%):** human review by default; **but** in a high-risk zone the default flips to "treat as foe / do not assume friendly" to protect critical assets.
- Risk-zone is operator-settable per area / proximity to friendly assets.
- Safety boundary unchanged: identification aid only; the dashboard recommends, the operator decides.

### Evaluation metrics

Need confusion matrix per state — precision, recall, false-positive rate (friendly jammed), false-negative rate (foe missed). Keep tight; do not let metrics scope explode.

### Demo plan refinement

- Source clip: drone race or drone battle footage with two visually-distinguishable drone types.
- Fine-tune YOLO on those two classes.
- MVP = visual classifier + marker detection on the source video.
- Pitch covers the multi-modal vision (audio, infrasound, ESP32 in commentary, not in code).

## Constraint update — 2026-05-02 late evening (post-meeting Birger SME ping — superseded above)

Birger surfaced a major new constraint after the meeting: **"assume jammed; most decision-making and identification must happen within the drone."** This is a real architecture pivot, not a tweak.

### Two video-stuff problems that we'd been mushing together

The meeting had quietly merged two distinct problems. The jamming constraint hits each one differently, which forced us to separate them:

- **Problem A — Self-identification.** A friendly drone stamps an authenticated marker into the video it broadcasts (the OSD layer). The drone marks; somebody else reads. *Original BlueMark concept.*
- **Problem B — Other-drone classification.** A drone's onboard camera sees other drones in the sky during flight; an onboard classifier labels them friend/foe/unknown. *Came in via Arpit's YOLO framing.*

These have different inputs, different consumers, and very different jamming exposure.

### How "assume jammed" affects each signal source

| Signal | Survives jamming? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| OSD marker — drone-side embedding (Problem A) | ✅ | Local computation; jamming-irrelevant |
| OSD marker — receiver-side reading | ⚠️ | Only when video reaches a friendly receiver |
| Visual classifier of other drones — onboard (Problem B) | ✅ | Forces edge compute (Coral / Jetson Nano-class) |
| Visual classifier — server-side | ❌ | Was implicitly assumed; jamming kills it |
| Bird's-eye stitch (multi-drone aggregation) | ❌ | Needs comms; demote to pitch-only |
| Acoustic / fiber / infrasound (ground sensors) | ❌ | Drone can't reach them; pitch-context only |

### Cost-tier implication for Sebastian

Sebastian's `docs/cost_tiers.md` framed BlueMark as Tier 0 (~$0/drone) software-first. Birger's constraint may force a *split* cost story: self-marking stays Tier 0, but onboard visual classification likely needs Tier 2 (microcontroller + NPU) or Tier 3 (Pi-class). ESP32 doesn't run YOLO. Worth flagging before the cost-tier pitch language locks.

### Safety-boundary tension (raised in urgent question 4)

Existing pitch language: *"Identification aid only. Human decision required."* If Birger's "decisions on the drone" means the *drone itself* consumes its own classification (autonomous action), that language no longer fits. Either the architecture has to keep a human somewhere in the loop, or the safety story has to be rewritten — Sebastian's call.
